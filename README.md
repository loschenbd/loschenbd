## What ties it together

The best products don't just store information or automate tasks. They help people feel oriented. They reduce friction, create momentum, and make better action feel obvious. Whether I'm working on enterprise software, a DTC reporting stack, a mobile app, or a health project, I'm usually chasing the same thing:

clarity people can use and adding a dash of delight... because we're human :).

For me, that usually means holding two truths at once: the operational truth in the numbers, and the human truth underneath them. If you miss either one, you can end up building somethingthat ultimately fails.

## How I work

I like products that are technically solid, simple to use, and a make people feel a spark of delightful magic. Clear and clever. Useful and impressive.

I can hang in the weeds with specs, schemas, edge cases, APIs, and workflow logic, but I care just as much about whether the thing feels human and creates meaningful value and experiences. Serious tools do not need to feel sterile.

I trust data deeply. I just don't think dashboards get the final vote. Especially in moments of real need, the felt experience is not a layer on top of the product. It is the product.
